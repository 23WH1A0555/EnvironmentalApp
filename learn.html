Recycling Education Center

How to Properly Sort Your Waste

Learn the basics of waste segregation to maximize recycling efficiency:

  • Plastics: Clean and dry containers, remove lids
  • Paper: Dry and uncontaminated by food
  • Glass: Separate by color if possible
  • Metal: Clean cans and foil
  • Organic: Compostable materials only

Why Recycling Matters

Recycling helps conserve natural resources, saves energy, reduces landfill waste, and decreases pollution. Each ton of recycled paper can save 17 trees and 7,000 gallons of water!

Recycling Process Explained

  1. Collection: Waste is gathered from homes and businesses
  2. Sorting: Materials are separated at recycling facilities
  3. Processing: Materials are cleaned and prepared
  4. Manufacturing: Recycled materials become new products
  5. Purchase: Consumers buy products made from recycled materials
